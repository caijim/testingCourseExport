To make a Unit graded, first you need to decide what assignment types you want to have in your course. You define your grading policy (including assignment types, weights, and numbers) on the Grading page in Studio. For example, you might decide to have 10 Homeworks (20% final grade, 1 droppable), 10 Reading Quizzes (20%), 1 Midterm (30%), and 1 Final (30%). To get to this page, scroll to the top of any Studio page and click the Settings menu, then Grading.

Once you've defined your assignment types, you need to mark your subsections as belonging to a certain assignment type. You do this from the Course Outline page. Click the gear icon next to the subsection title:

Then pick an assignment type in the popup (and assign a due date, if you like) and click Save. Everything in that subsection will count as one assignment of the chosen assignment type.

Go ahead and set up your grading policy on the Grading page, then mark a subsection as graded.

If you plan on offering a Statement of Accomplishment with your course, please go to Part 3.5: Statements of Accomplishment. Or, if you won't be offering an SoA, go ahead to Part 4: Files & Uploads.
